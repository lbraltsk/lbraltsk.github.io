Biography

I am a fourth-year undergraduate at Macalester College in St. Paul, MN. My degree is in mathematics and physics with an astronomy emphasis. My research interests include galaxy formation/evolution, data mining of large surveys, and astronomical instrumentation, particularly at radio frequencies. Please see my CV for more information on my academic background.

When I'm not in class or working on research, you might find me running around the Twin Cities, sharing my love of the universe, building high power rockets, or supporting Minnesota United FC. I can occasionally be counted on to tweet @lbraltsk. I was also a regular columnist for ROOKIE magazine for over four years, contributing personal essays, advice, and featured playlists.

Recent

  • June--August 2019: I am currently a visiting summer student at the Netherlands Institute for Radio Astronomy (ASTRON), where I am imaging and analyzing ultra-compact high-velocity clouds in order to better understand their fundamental properties and role in populating the Local Group.
  • January 2019: I attended the 233rd meeting of the American Astronomical Society, where I presented a poster on our recent ApJL publication: "Delayed Stellar Mass Assembly in the Low Surface Brightness Dwarf Galaxy KDG 215."
  • August 2018: I published a work in the ApJ Letters (5th author): "Delayed Stellar Mass Assembly in the Low Surface Brightness Dwarf Galaxy KDG215." Preprint available here: arXiv:1808.07108
  • August 2018: I attended the 5th La Serena School for Data Science at the AURA Observatory complex in La Serena, Chile—see our group cameo on CTIO Twitter!
