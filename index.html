I am a third-year undergraduate student of physics, astronomy, and mathematics at Macalester College in Saint Paul, MN. My primary research interests include galaxy evolution and astronomical instrumentation, particularly at radio and millimeter/submillimeter wavelengths.

Most recently, I was a summer NSF REU student at the University of Michigan, where I studied neutrino physics with Prof. Joshua Spitz and graduate student Johnathon Jordan. My role was to simulate cosmic ray events for the upcoming liquid scintillator neutrino experiment JSNS2 in order to better differentiate "false positives" signals from real detections.

In my spare time I run, dance, dream about getting my private pilot's license, and support Minnesota United FC.
